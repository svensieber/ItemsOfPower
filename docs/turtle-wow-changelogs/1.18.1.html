HUNTER

Ranged variant of Hunter has been one of the least changed classes in Class Changes 2 and has fallen behind other damage dealers and melee Hunter not only in raw DPS, but also in terms of gameplay.
Ranged Hunter is extremely timing-dependent and offers little reactivity in its rotation. In this patch, we are reviewing the Hunter kit as a whole to ensure more abilities are useful and impactful, and reworking the talent trees to make them more engaging in endgame content.

General
New Spell: Aspect of the Viper - Learned at Level 56.

"The hunter takes on the aspect of the viper, regenerating 5% of maximum mana every 5 second. Only one Aspect can be active at a time."

(No Mana Cost)

Developer Comment: Hunter is a class that cannot reliably regain mana without proper set bonuses and can be quite mana-hungry. To offset this, we are adding a passive way to regain mana with the small drawback of occupying an Aspect slot.
Trueshot Aura: Now baseline, learned at Level 40.

"Increases the attack power of party members within 45 yards by 3%, plus an additional 30."

Developer Comment: Trueshot Aura was a design-limiting ability and an uninteresting capstone. For that reason, we are making it baseline for all Hunters in a slightly toned-down version.
Steady Shot: Now baseline, learned at Level 20.

Developer Comment: Steady Shot has been a must-have ability for all ranged Hunters, while Aimed Shot was rendered almost useless by its existence. We believe such a core ability should be part of the baseline kit, so we are returning Steady Shot as one.
Volley: Has seen various changes.
- Now a 3 second cast instead of a continuous channeled ability.
- Mana Costs reduced:
Rank 1 (Level 40): From 350 to 210
Rank 2 (Level 50): From 420 to 270
Rank 3 (Level 60): From 490 to 330
- Now has Attack Power scaling:
Rank 1 (Level 40): 4% Attack Power Scaling
Rank 2 (Level 50): 5% Attack Power Scaling
Rank 3 (Level 60): 6% Attack Power Scaling
Any spell power scaling remains unchanged.

Developer Comment: Volley has always been interesting in concept but too weak to justify use in practice. Due to its channeled nature, its damage would have had to be increased to unreasonable levels to be viable. We are therefore converting it into a casted ability that does not require the Hunter to stop performing other actions for 10 seconds to reach its full potential.
Arcane Shot: Base damage values reduced but now scales with ranged weapon damage.

Damage Values:

Rank 2 (Level 12): Damage reduced from 21 to 17, added 15% ranged weapon damage.

ROGUE

We’re generally happy with the recent changes to Rogue, and community feedback shows that the class is largely performing as intended. That said, in reviewing data across multiple environments, we've identified that Rogue is currently a bit overperformant in certain areas.

To address this, we're making a few targeted numerical adjustments aimed at bringing performance back in line without changing how the class fundamentally plays.

General
Poisons: Reduced scaling at lower ranks:

Corrosive Poison:

Dissolvent Poison:

Developer Comment: Early access to higher poison scaling was resulting in stronger-than-intended performance during leveling and early gearing. This change smooths out that curve while preserving endgame effectiveness.
Blade Flurry: Reduced Energy regeneration penalty from 30% to 20%.

Developer Comment: Blade Flurry should feel like a powerful cleave tool without overly punishing resource flow. This adjustment helps improve its usability while keeping its overall power in check.

Physical DPS Enhancement will see some small tweaks in patch 1.18.1. Tank Enhancement and spell-power–focused Enhancement, however, are getting more meaningful updates to their core talents. These changes are aimed at strengthening each playstyle’s identity and reducing overlap with similar mechanics found in other classes.

General

Lightning Shield: Mana cost reduced across the board by roughly 25%
Rank 1 (Level 8): 45 -> 40
Rank 2 (Level 16): 80 -> 70
Rank 3 (Level 24): 125 -> 105
Rank 4 (Level 32): 180 -> 150
Rank 5 (Level 40): 240 -> 200
Rank 6 (Level 48): 305 -> 255

Enhancement

Stormstrike: Can no-longer roll and trigger Windfury Weapon's effect twice.
Windfury Weapon: Each hit now has a 25% chance (was 20%).
All of the following changes are to affect Elemental Weapons and its sub-effects:

Windfury: Reworked to the following effect: “Gaining extra attacks increases your attack speed by 1% for 5 sec. Stacks up to 2/4/6 times.”

Flametongue: Reworked to the following effect: “Hitting enemies with your attacks increases the damage done by your Fire Totems and spells by 10/20/30% for 5 sec.”

Developer Comment: Requiring the Shaman to actively engage in melee combat to keep the Fire damage increase active should make this effect lean more towards spell oriented Enhancement Shaman gameplay while requiring pure Fire damage Elemental Shamans to stay in melee range to utilize its full power.

Rockbiter: Reworked to the following effect: “Your physical damage builds up an earthen bulwark for 20% of the damage dealt, tripled while you're wearing a shield. The earthen bulwark absorbs 5/10/15% of incoming damage for 8 seconds or until the bulwark mitigates enough damage. The bulwark's durability cannot exceed 20% of your maximum health.”

Developer Comment: Changing the Rockbiter Weapon effect from a passive damage reduction into an active component that's kept up by attacking should feel make it feel more active in active usage while deterring non-Enhancement specializations from using the effect as a passive benefit which led to them being more durable, especially in Player vs Player environments.
Flurry: Reworked to the following effect “Increases your attack speed by 8/11/14/17/20% for your next 3 swings after dealing a critical strike.”

Developer Comment: This change should increase the uptime of Flurry especially for spell oriented Enhancement Shaman gameplay, making it less punishing to have less physical critical strike chance compared to their physical oriented counterpart.
Lightning Strike: Water Shield's Attack Power-to-Mana ratio changed from 20:1 to 18:1.

Developer Comment: Shields are central to our vision of Enhancement, they are the specialization's biggest sink of mana, expecially in contexts where AOE damage is involved. This should improve the dependability of the specialization's mana bar.


Elemental

Eye of the Storm: Renamed to Call of Earth:

"Earth Shield gains 1/2 additional charges and grants an additional 35/70% chance to avoid interruption while casting a spell."
Elemental Fury: Now also affects Frost spells.
Earthquake: Base damage values increased by 15%, cooldown reduced from 20 seconds to 18 seconds.
New Damage Values:
Rank 1 (Level 40): 227-253 -> 262-291
Rank 2 (Level 50): 343-387 -> 395-446
Rank 3 (Level 60): 510-551 -> 587-634

Developer Comment: When designing a class, we often look for ways to blend flavor with function. While this frequently involves fitting a square peg into a triangular slot, Elemental is particularly well suited to aligning form and function when enough creativity is applied. Proper spell pushback resistance has long been missing from Elemental, as Eye of the Storm saw little use in either PvP or PvE: in the former, critical hits occurred infrequently enough that the talent was often skipped, while in the latter, critical hits were almost always fatal regardless. As a result, we’ve decided to retire the talent in favor of a solution that better serves its intended purpose, while also reinforcing Elemental’s thematic identity. With this change, the Elemental tree now answers the call of Earth, Flame, and Thunder—now if only we could find a way to introduce the Call of Wind in time for the 21st of September.

Although we generally view Elemental Shields as a concept better suited to Enhancement gameplay, and Water Shield as central to Restoration’s mana sustain, we are happy to give Earth Shield a place within the Elemental specialization. This allows Elemental shamans to make more meaningful use of the Earth element beyond its capstone talent. Note that the bonus from Earth’s Poise, the buff granted by this talent, stacks additively with other sources of spell pushback reduction.

The changes to Earthquake are intended to elevate the spell into a capstone worthy not just in form, but in function. While the adjustments themselves are straightforward, our goal is to encourage shamans to use the spell in a wider range of scenarios, addressing the fact that its original damage values often felt underwhelming.


Tier Bonus Changes
  • Restoration Shaman Tier 1 (3 Set): Changed description to clarify the totem radius is increased by 10 yards and not set to 30 yards.
  • Restoration Shaman Tier 1 (8 Set): Reworked to the following: "Your Healing Wave and Lesser Healing Wave have a 25% chance to surround the target with an earth wall, absorbing 250 damage. Lasts 6 sec.".
As a final note, the Shaman class development team would like to sincerely thank the small, close-knit Shaman community. The thoughtful feedback shared on community servers and forums has been incredibly valuable, and it has played an important role alongside our usual log analysis work. We're confident it will continue to be valuable for the future development of the class as a whole.

Fury
Improved Whirlwind: Renamed to Ravager and now has an additional effect:
"Reduces the cooldown of Whirlwind by 1/1.5/2 seconds and rage cost of Cleave by 1/2/3."

Developer Comment: Talent points could previously be spent before Whirlwind was learned, which caused confusion for new players. We’ve added a small rage cost Reduction to cleave to fix this inconsistency without affecting balance in a significant way.
Cast time reduction granted to Slam by Flurry has been corrected. For example, with 5 points spent in Flurry, Slam cast time will now properly be 1.92 seconds instead of 1.75 seconds.

Developer Comment: In the previous patch, Flurry was updated to reduce Slam cast time, but an incorrect formula resulted in more reduction than intended.


Arms
Swapped position of Boundless Anger and Precision Cut talents.
Precision Cut: Changed to the following:
"Increases the damage done per extra rage point spent on Execute by 25/50/75%."

Developer Comment: The previous redesign of Precision Cut did not deliver satisfying Execute gameplay and made the ability feel underwhelming. Restoring increased damage per Rage spent allows Execute to better fulfill its role as a high-impact finisher for Arms Warriors.


Protection
Defiance: Changed to:
"Increases the threat generated while in Defensive Stance by an additional 4/8/12/15/20%."
Shield Slam: Attack Power scaling increased from 15% to 20% and its additonal threat modifier is increased from 50% to 75%.

Developer Comment: Protection Warriors have been falling behind other tanks. These changes provide targeted improvements, while a more comprehensive review of the Protection talent tree and overall kit is planned for the future.
